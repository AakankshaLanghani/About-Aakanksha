projects

AI Financial Analyzer is a full-stack AI platform for business financial analysis. The pitch is simple: upload an Excel file, ask questions in plain English (things like "which city had the highest GP%?" or "how many customers are overdue?"), and download a professionally formatted multi-page PDF report with KPIs, charts, and customer tables, instantly. Built with React, FastAPI, and GPT-4o-mini. The key design principle is that the LLM never touches the numbers. All aggregations, rankings, and percentage calculations run deterministically in pandas. The model only writes the business-language explanation from pre-computed results, which means the numbers are always right. Deployed on Vercel and Hugging Face Spaces.

AI Hiring Assessment Generator is a SaaS frontend for generating AI-powered MCQ hiring assessments straight from job descriptions. Paste a JD, pick a difficulty level, and get a categorised set of questions across Logical, Analytical, Role-Based, and Situational sections, exportable as PDF or DOCX. Built with Next.js 14, TypeScript, and Tailwind CSS. I added a mock fallback so the whole frontend works independently of the backend, which turned out to be a good call during demos.

ATS Resume Analyzer is an AI-powered resume screening tool built for HR teams. Upload up to 10 resumes, paste a job description, and get a ranked candidate table with honest, human-like scoring. The scoring engine does skill equivalence detection (CapCut = Premiere, that kind of thing), soft skill inference, retention risk flagging, and generates suggested interview questions per candidate. It supports OpenAI, Groq (free tier), Anthropic, and local Ollama for teams that need full privacy. Nothing is stored or logged. Built with React, Vite, and FastAPI.

research

EEG-Based Emotion Recognition System for Clinical Assistive Healthcare   2025
Aakanksha Pardeep

The problem this addresses is a real and underappreciated one: patients with locked-in syndrome, end-stage ALS, or severe spinal cord injury are fully cognitively aware but have no way to express whether they are in pain, scared, or uncomfortable. Standard ICU equipment tracks haemodynamics just fine but offers nothing on subjective distress.

I built an end-to-end Brain-Computer Interface for real-time emotion recognition from EEG signals. The system processes 14-channel data from the GAMEEMO database (28 subjects, Emotiv EPOC+ headset, 128 Hz), runs it through four-stage filtering (band-pass, notch, ICA, epoch normalisation), and extracts 11 features per epoch across five canonical frequency bands using a 50% overlap sliding window. Six classifiers were trained on 13,068 windowed samples across 4 emotion classes: SVM, KNN, Decision Tree, Random Forest, CNN, and XGBoost. XGBoost won by 5.4 percentage points over its nearest competitor, hitting 90% overall accuracy and 91.5% precision on the Stress/Pain class, which is the one that matters most clinically. The alert system fires only when prediction confidence clears 85%, a threshold set deliberately to keep false alarms low in environments already suffering from alert fatigue. Feature importance analysis confirmed the model is picking up on real neurophysiology: the most discriminative signals come from frontal Beta and Gamma channels, consistent with the affective computing literature.
